Lace knitting pattern utilize the continuity of a thread forming two dimensional form out of one dimensional medium. Topological code of knots, intersections and loosening provide the difference in material density.

One of the first iterations of the thread manufacturing. The thread is floated away from the surface building loose support for outer layers. So the floating can be achieved through multiple layer offsetting from the original framework.

Three tool paths with different level of material freedom. Each tool path allow the material to reveal its tectonic pattern.

Three iterations have different level of material freedom through speed and proximity to the framework.

The diagram shows two main stages of the process. The development stage revealed the tectonic patterns of the fabrication process. And the design stage where the patterns were used as a medium to create a form. The design process then would have necessary amount of iterations. Each iteration is optically captured and modeled to inform new iteration.

3D scan capture of the second iteration of the design stage. This scan is used to build a new tool path on top of it

The result of the second stage of design iteration. Due to the lack of robot lab time our group 3d printed an outer frame instead of building it from the core. However in case of more lab freedom the outer frame should be formed floating the threads of the core of the object.
